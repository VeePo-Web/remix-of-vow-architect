## Global Settings — Apply to EVERY Clip

These rules ensure all 8 clips feel like one unbroken continuous shot, not 8 separate videos stitched together.

### Technical
- **Aspect ratio:** 3:2 (1280×854)
- **Duration:** As noted per act
- **Frame rate:** 24fps
- **Mode:** Professional / Cinematic quality

### Camera (SAME for all clips)
- **Movement:** Slow, steady forward dolly — always moving forward, never stopping, never panning sideways, never pulling back
- **Speed:** Approximately 1-2 feet per second of drift — ceremonial, unhurried, processional
- **Height:** Eye level (~5'6") — first-person POV of someone walking down an aisle
- **Lens:** 35mm anamorphic — shallow depth of field, subtle horizontal flare, oval bokeh
- **Stabilization:** Perfectly smooth glide — no handheld shake, no bounce, like a Steadicam or dolly on rails

### Color & Grade (SAME for all clips)
- **Color science:** Warm bias throughout — no cool tones, no blue shadows, no teal
- **Shadows:** Always warm charcoal or deep amber, never pure neutral black (except ACT I void)
- **Highlights:** Cream and gold, never blown to pure clinical white — always warm
- **Film grain:** Consistent fine grain across all clips — 35mm film stock texture, not digital noise
- **Contrast:** Medium-low — soft, lifted blacks (except ACT I), no harsh contrast
- **Saturation:** Restrained and elegant — rich but never vivid or punchy

### Continuity Rules
- **Light direction:** The primary light source is always AHEAD of camera — the viewer walks toward light. Light grows brighter and warmer with each successive act
- **Depth of field:** Foreground elements (fabric, particles) are always soft bokeh. Mid-ground is sharpest. Background falls to soft focus
- **Particles:** Gold dust motes / pollen / sparkles appear starting ACT II and increase in density every act. By ACT VIII they are everywhere. They must look IDENTICAL across all clips — same size, same color (warm gold), same float speed
- **Fabric:** Sheer ivory/white tulle, organza, or veil fabric appears starting ACT III and increases in presence. Same fabric type, same color, same translucency across all clips — it should look like the same physical material throughout
- **No hard edges:** Every element enters and exits frame gradually — nothing pops in or out. Soft transitions only
- **No people:** No faces, no hands, no body parts visible. The silhouette in ACT VIII is the ONLY human form and it must remain an abstract dark shape against light
- **No text, logos, or UI elements** in any frame
- **No sound design needed** — video will be muted

### Mood Arc (the emotional throughline)
The entire video is ONE emotion building: **anticipation → arrival.** A bride walking toward her vow. Each act is a step closer. The light grows. The world opens. The beauty intensifies. By the end, the viewer has arrived at something sacred. There is no conflict, no tension (except the brief shadow in ACT V), no drama — just a slow, inevitable, beautiful approach toward light.

### How to Use These Prompts
1. Generate each act's start and end keyframes in NanoBanana using the image prompts above
2. Feed the start/end keyframes into Kling 3.0 as image-to-video with the corresponding video prompt below
3. Each clip's ending frame should match the next clip's starting frame exactly — this is what creates seamless continuity
4. After all 8 clips are generated, concatenate with 0.5s crossfade transitions

## Post-Production Notes

After generating all 8 clips in Kling 3.0:

1. **Concatenate** all 8 clips into one seamless 76-second video (use crossfade transitions of ~0.5s between clips)
2. **Re-encode** with all I-frames for scroll scrubbing:
   ```
   ffmpeg -i concatenated.mp4 -c:v libx264 -preset slow -crf 23 -g 1 -keyint_min 1 -an -vf "scale=1280:854" weddings-full.mp4
   ```
3. **Remove audio** (the video is muted on the site, scroll-driven)
4. **Target file size:** Under 80MB
5. **First frame** must be pure black (matching ACT I void)
6. **No hard cuts** — each act should flow seamlessly into the next
