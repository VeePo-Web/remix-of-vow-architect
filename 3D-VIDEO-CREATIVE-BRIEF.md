# 3D Video Creative Brief — The Sacred Scroll

## For the 3D Production Team

This document contains the exact copy for each section of the /weddings scroll experience, the narrative purpose behind each section, and a detailed visual direction for the 3D video that plays behind it. The video is one continuous piece — no cuts, no scene breaks. It plays from 0:00 to approximately 1:16, scrubbed by the user's scroll position.

---

## Overall Visual Direction

### Theme: "The Walk Down the Aisle"

The video is not the inside of a piano. It is the emotional journey of a bride walking toward her vow — told through light, texture, and atmosphere. The viewer IS the bride. The camera is her perspective.

### Color Arc

| Section | Time | Palette |
|---------|------|---------|
| ACT I | 0:00–0:08 | **Pure black.** No color. Void. |
| ACT II | 0:08–0:18 | **Charcoal + single warm flame.** A candle in darkness. |
| ACT III | 0:18–0:28 | **Warm amber + soft ivory.** The flame grows. Fabric textures emerge. |
| ACT IV | 0:28–0:37 | **Gold + cream.** Morning light through sheer curtains. |
| ACT V | 0:37–0:44 | **Rich gold + ivory.** Bright, warm, confident. |
| ACT VI | 0:44–0:56 | **White + gold + subtle blush.** Outdoor ceremony light. |
| ACT VII | 0:56–1:04 | **Full bright white + gold sparkle.** Sunlight through petals. |
| ACT VIII | 1:04–1:16 | **Ethereal white + gold + veil.** Standing before the silhouette. |

### Key Visual Motifs

- **The Flame** — A single candle flame appears in ACT I and grows throughout. By ACT V it has become a field of warm light. By ACT VIII it is pure radiance.
- **Fabric/Veil** — Sheer, flowing fabric (tulle, organza, silk) appears subtly in ACT III and becomes more prominent. By ACT VIII it IS the frame — a veil surrounding the viewer.
- **Particles** — Tiny gold particles (dust motes, floating petals, sparkles) increase density from ACT IV onward. By ACT VIII they are everywhere.
- **The Silhouette** — In the final frames (ACT VIII), the suggestion of a figure standing in light. Not detailed. A silhouette. The bride — or the person waiting at the altar. Ambiguous. Sacred.
- **Outdoor light** — From ACT IV onward, the light should feel like it is coming from outside — through trees, through fabric, through open air. This is an outdoor mountain wedding.

### Camera Feel

Slow, steady forward drift. The viewer is walking forward. Always forward. Never backward, never sideways. The pace matches the scroll — deliberate, unhurried, ceremonial.

## Section-by-Section: Copy + Story + Visual Direction

### ACT I — THE HOOK
**Video time: 0:00 – 0:08 (8 seconds)**
**Scroll: 0% – 10.5%**

#### Copy

```
PARKER GAWRYLETZ
Ceremony Pianist

——✦——

You are about to stand
before everyone you love
and make a promise.

——✦——
```

#### Story Purpose

This is the hook. The visitor has just arrived. They are likely engaged, stressed, and overwhelmed with decisions. This section does not sell. It does not even mention piano. It speaks to the truth of their moment: you are about to make a promise. This creates immediate relevance and emotional connection. Parker's name appears in the void like a signature on a dark page — establishing identity with confidence and restraint.

#### Visual Direction

**Pure black void.** Nothing visible for the first 2–3 seconds. Then: a single point of warm light appears. A candle flame. Small. Quiet. Just barely there. It does not illuminate the room — it illuminates the possibility. The flame grows slowly, just enough to suggest a warm glow in the darkness. By the end of this section, the flame has a soft halo but the surrounding space is still dark.

**Mood:** Stillness. Anticipation. The moment before the moment.

### ACT II — MY PROCESS
**Video time: 0:08 – 0:18 (10 seconds)**
**Scroll: 10.5% – 23.7%**

#### Copy

```
——— MY PROCESS ———

The music at your ceremony
will not be left to chance.

It begins months before.

I start with one question:
What song was playing
when you knew?

From there, I learn
what you want to feel
the moment you begin your walk —
and what silence needs protecting.

——✦——
```

#### Story Purpose

This is where trust begins. The copy does three things: (1) eliminates the fear of the unknown ("will not be left to chance"), (2) demonstrates depth of care ("begins months before"), and (3) asks a question so personal that the reader immediately feels understood ("What song was playing when you knew?"). The final line — "what silence needs protecting" — is the expert moment. It shows Parker thinks about things no one else would. This builds authority through empathy, not credentials.

#### Visual Direction

The candle flame from ACT I is now slightly larger. It sits at center frame. Around it, the faintest suggestion of a space begins to emerge — perhaps the edge of a table, or the soft fold of fabric. The light is still predominantly amber/gold on charcoal. Dust motes float through the candlelight. The texture of the world is just beginning to become tangible — like waking up in a dimly lit room. The camera drifts slowly forward.

**Mood:** Intimacy. A quiet room. A conversation before everything begins.

### ACT III — YOUR MUSIC
**Video time: 0:18 – 0:28 (10 seconds)**
**Scroll: 23.7% – 36.8%**

#### Copy

```
——— YOUR MUSIC ———

Then I disappear
into your story.

Your ceremony music is not pulled
from a playlist.
It is composed from our conversation —
note by note, for the two of you.

I send you a first draft.
You shape it.
We refine it together until it sounds
exactly as you imagined.

Every vow spoken
becomes sacred
the moment it is heard.
```

#### Story Purpose

This section has two movements. The first half is about the craft — showing the collaborative process, the bespoke composition, the refinement. "Not pulled from a playlist" directly addresses the fear that their ceremony will be generic. The second half — "Every vow spoken / becomes sacred / the moment it is heard" — is the emotional climax of the first half of the page. It is not about Parker anymore. It is about the vow itself. This is the moment the visitor feels the weight of their own decision. It creates the gravity that makes the rest of the page feel earned.

#### Visual Direction

The warm amber light is now rich and full. The candle has become a broader source of warmth — like early morning light coming through sheer curtains or a tent. Fabric textures are now clearly visible: the soft drape of tulle or organza, catching the light. Gold undertones emerge. The space feels like a bridal suite at dawn — intimate, warm, sacred. As the vow moment text appears, the light intensifies. A soft bloom of pure white begins to push through the golden warmth. The veil motif appears for the first time — a sheet of translucent fabric drifting slowly across part of the frame.

**Mood:** Rising warmth. Preparation becoming ceremony. Dawn becoming morning.

### ACT IV — ABOUT
**Video time: 0:28 – 0:37 (9 seconds)**
**Scroll: 36.8% – 48.7%**

#### Copy

```
——✦——

——— ABOUT ———

I take five weddings
a year.

Not because I have to.
Because yours deserves
my full attention.

While other pianists juggle dozens
of bookings, every couple I work with
receives months of devoted preparation.
Not a template.
A score written for two people.

500+ ceremonies · SOCAN licensed · $4M insured

[Begin the Conversation →]
```

#### Story Purpose

This is the authority section. The "five weddings a year" is the scarcity anchor — it immediately signals premium positioning. "Not because I have to. Because yours deserves my full attention." turns scarcity into a gift rather than a boast. The contrast ("While other pianists juggle dozens...") is the Russell Brunson competitor reframe — it names the alternative without naming names, and makes the premium choice feel obvious. The trust badges (500+ ceremonies, SOCAN, $4M insured) provide the rational justification for the emotional decision they are already making. The CTA appears for the first time — soft, invitational: "Begin the Conversation."

#### Visual Direction

The scene has shifted from interior to the threshold of outdoors. The light is now coming from outside — warm, golden morning light streaming through an opening. Perhaps the doorway of a tent, or the edge of a veranda looking out onto a mountain landscape. The fabric/veil motif continues — sheer drapes frame the edges of the frame, catching the light. The first tiny gold particles appear — like pollen or dust motes in sunlight. The space is opening up. The world outside is bright and warm. The camera continues its slow forward drift, approaching the outdoor light.

**Mood:** Confidence. Openness. The morning of the wedding. Stepping into the light.

### ACT V — THE PROMISE
**Video time: 0:37 – 0:44 (7 seconds)**
**Scroll: 48.7% – 57.9%**

#### Copy

```
——✦——

——— THE PROMISE ———

You have a fear you have
not said out loud yet.

"What if the pianist treats
our ceremony like just another
Saturday booking?"

That will never happen here.
Because I do not start
with a setlist.
I start with your story.
```

#### Story Purpose

This is the fear kill — the most important section from a conversion standpoint. It names the fear they have been carrying silently ("you have not said out loud yet") which creates an almost eerie feeling of being understood. Then it voices the specific fear in their own internal language: "just another Saturday booking." This is the objection every premium service must destroy. The answer is absolute: "That will never happen here." Not "probably won't" or "I try not to." It is a guarantee made through conviction, not legal language. The final line — "I start with your story" — circles back to the process section, creating cohesion.

#### Visual Direction

This section needs visual tension followed by resolution. The golden outdoor light from ACT IV is still present, but a subtle shadow passes through — like a cloud briefly dimming the sun, or a veil falling over the camera. This visual "dip" mirrors the fear in the text. Then, as "That will never happen here" appears, the shadow lifts. The light returns brighter than before. The gold is richer. The particles increase. The resolution is visual as well as verbal — the shadow was named and it was banished.

**Mood:** Vulnerability → Reassurance. Shadow → Light. The moment of doubt resolved.

#### Story Purpose

This is the offer. After the emotional arc of the first five sections, the visitor is ready for specifics. The opening line — "Everything is handled. It is the promise of peace of mind, which is what the bride at 2am googling "wedding pianist" actually wants. The pricing is transparent and tiered — three clear options with emotional framing ("the most sacred hour of your life," "your first moments as married," "the last glass raised"). The second CTA appears: "Hold My Date."

The camera has now fully emerged into outdoor light. This is the ceremony space. Bright, warm, open. The Rockies or rolling hills visible in soft focus beyond. Sheer fabric panels (like ceremony draping) frame the sides of the shot. Sunlight pours through. The gold particles are abundant — floating, catching light. For the pricing section, the visual should feel spacious and still — like standing in the center of a decorated ceremony site, looking forward toward the altar. The light is white-gold, clean, confident. No shadows. Pure assurance.

**Mood:** Clarity. Confidence. The ceremony site is ready. Everything is in place.

### ACT VII — TESTIMONIALS
**Video time: 0:56 – 1:04 (8 seconds)**
**Scroll: 73.7% – 84.2%**

#### Copy

```
——✦——

——— TESTIMONIALS ———

The music stayed
with them.

"He played the song I walked down
the aisle to — and I forgot

— Sarah & James
Priddis

"Our guests still talk about
the music. Not the food.
Not the flowers. The music."

— Emily & David

```

#### Story Purpose

Social proof. After the offer, the visitor needs validation that this is real. Two testimonials, both specific and emotionally devastating. Sarah's quote — "I forgot there were a hundred people watching" — communicates the highest possible compliment: the music was so perfectly matched to the moment that the entire room disappeared. Emily's quote — "Not the food. Not the flowers. The music." — reframes what matters at a wedding. It positions the music not as a vendor but as the memory. Real names. Real locations. No stock photos, no anonymity.

#### Visual Direction

The light is now at its brightest — pure white with warm gold highlights. The ceremony is in full bloom. Flower petals float through the air among the gold particles. The veil/fabric motif is now everywhere — draped, flowing, catching the light. This should feel like the most beautiful moment of the ceremony — the moment just after the vow, when everyone is still holding their breath. Ethereal. Luminous. The camera continues its forward drift, approaching something just out of view.

**Mood:** Joy. Reverence. The warmth of proof. This is real. This happened.

### ACT VIII — THE CLOSE
**Video time: 1:04 – 1:16 (12 seconds)**
**Scroll: 84.2% – 100%**

#### Copy

```
——✦——

If you ask me:

"Do you promise to hold
the tension of the sacred
and the celebration?

Both the hope
and the depth?"

I will answer:

I do.

[Hold My Date →]

Response within 24 hours. Always.
```

#### Story Purpose

This is the close, and it is the most important piece of copywriting on the entire page. It mirrors the structure of a wedding vow. The visitor has been reading about Parker's process, his care, his commitment — and now he is making his own vow directly to them. "Do you promise to hold the tension of the sacred and the celebration?" is the question every couple secretly wishes they could ask their vendors: will you treat this with the weight it deserves? "Both the hope and the depth?" acknowledges that weddings are not just happy — they are emotional, complex, layered with family history and future promises. And then: "I do." Two words. The same two words they will say to each other. Parker's commitment mirrors theirs. The CTA and trust line ("Response within 24 hours. Always.") hold permanently — they never fade. This is the last thing the visitor sees on the page, and it stays.

#### Visual Direction

**This is the climax of the entire visual journey.** The camera passes through a final curtain of sheer fabric — veil or organza — and enters pure light. The silhouette of a figure appears in the center of the frame: a bride, standing in a cascade of veil, surrounded by golden sparkles and white light. The figure is not detailed — it is a silhouette, a shape, a suggestion. Sacred. The veil frames everything. The particles are dense — almost like stars. The background is pure white-gold luminance with soft ivory edges. As "I do." appears on screen, the figure in the veil is at its most visible. The camera has stopped moving. It has arrived. The journey is complete.

**The final frame should be: White-gold light. Veil fabric. A silhouette. Stillness.**

This frame holds. The "I do." holds. The CTA holds. The trust line holds. Nothing fades. The page ends in an eternal moment of arrival.

**Mood:** Transcendence. Arrival. The answer to everything. Sacred stillness.

## Technical Notes for 3D Production

- **Total duration:** ~76 seconds (8 sections, continuous)
- **Resolution:** 1280×854 minimum (current encode is this size)
- **Codec:** H.264, **ALL I-FRAMES** (every frame must be a keyframe for scroll scrubbing)
- **File target:** Under 80MB total for web delivery
- **Frame rate:** 24fps
- **Audio:** None (muted video, scroll-driven)
- **The video must flow seamlessly** — no hard cuts between sections. Each section should transition smoothly into the next through light, color, and texture changes.
- **The poster image** (first frame of the video) should be pure black — matching the void of ACT I.
