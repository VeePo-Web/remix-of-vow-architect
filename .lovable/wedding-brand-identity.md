## 2. Non-Negotiables, Flex, and Dealbreakers

### Non-Negotiables (what cannot change)
- First-person voice ("I will," "I understand," "I arrive") across every touchpoint
- The selectivity positioning: 5-10 weddings per year, each one receiving months of preparation
- The collaborative process as the core differentiator: song curation and refinement rounds beginning months before the ceremony
- Full musical choreography of the ceremony --- every cue mapped, every transition planned
- Universal sacredness: honoring the gravity of marriage without religious exclusion
- Quiet luxury aesthetic: no bright colors, no shout, no commodity signals
- The Death/Life emotional arc: every page breathes between vigil and celebration
- The semicolon (;) as sacred brand object --- threshold between promise and fulfillment
- Verb-forward CTAs: "Hold my date," "Begin the conversation," "Tell me your story"
- The tagline "'Til Death; Unto Life" is a covenant, not a slogan
- The mission: "To let my music sound like what your hearts feel like"
- "500+ events" --- never "500+ weddings"
- No Banff Mode references of any kind

### Allowed Flex
- Specific copy phrasing can evolve as long as tone and voice rules hold
- Image selection can rotate seasonally (winter mountain ceremonies vs summer garden)
- Package names and pricing can adjust without affecting brand identity
- The ratio of dark-to-light sections can shift based on page context
- Genre categories can expand as repertoire grows
- The number of refinement rounds in the process can vary by couple

### Dealbreakers (instant brand dilution)
- Using "we" instead of "I" --- this is a sole practitioner brand built on personal trust
- Any language positioning Parker as a "vendor," "entertainer," "performer," or "service provider"
- Stock photography of generic happy couples
- Countdown timers, urgency tactics, or manufactured scarcity
- Bright, saturated color accents or playful typography
- Competitor comparison tables framed as attack marketing
- Any CTA using "Book," "Submit," "Sign Up," or "Get Started"
- Any reference to Banff Mode, Banff venues, or Parks Canada compliance
- Claiming "500+ weddings" or "500+ ceremonies"

With 17 years at the piano, 6 years performing professionally, and 500+ events behind him, Parker occupies the only position in Southern Alberta where deep personal connection, months-long refinement, and full ceremony sound direction converge. In a market where DJs run playlists, musicians show up and play covers, and couples are tempted to skip live music entirely, Parker's work begins not at the ceremony but in the first conversation --- and doesn't end until the last guest has left.

### Category Name You Own
**Ceremony Sound Direction** --- not "wedding pianist," not "ceremony musician." A sound director who begins collaborating months before anyone walks down the aisle.

### Unique Mechanisms
1. **Immediate Collaboration** --- Work begins the week of booking, even if the wedding is 8 months away. No other local musician starts this early.
2. **Collaborative Song Curation** --- The couple and Parker build the ceremony soundtrack together over weeks and months, not from a preset list.
3. **Refinement Rounds** --- Multiple iterations where the couple hears progress, gives feedback, and guides the direction until the sound matches their vision.
4. **Full Ceremony Musical Choreography** --- Every entrance, reading, vow, ring exchange, and exit is mapped to a specific musical cue with intentional transitions.
5. **Run-of-Show Documentation** --- A co-signed PDF cue sheet that choreographs every musical moment of the ceremony.
6. **Triple Redundancy** --- Backup equipment and printed cue sheets. Nothing left to chance.
7. **SPL Logging** --- Sound pressure level readings taken and delivered post-ceremony as proof of audibility.

## 9. Visual Identity Direction

### Visual Principles (tied to Brand Spine)
1. **Inhale/Exhale Rhythm** --- Dark sections (vigil, preparation, weight) alternate with light sections (covenant, joy, hope). The page breathes like a ceremony.
2. **Cinematic Depth** --- Every section has atmospheric layers: background image at low opacity, grain, fog, vignette, warm glow. Nothing is flat. Everything has dimension.
3. **Sacred Objects** --- The semicolon (;), the golden diamond, the golden thread. These are the brand's visual punctuation marks. Used sparingly. Never decorative.
4. **Vast Whitespace** --- 80px+ section gaps. Content breathes. What is absent communicates as powerfully as what is present.
5. **Warm Light as Metaphor** --- Vow-yellow appears only at threshold moments: CTAs, underlines beneath sacred words, the semicolon. It represents the candle flame, the held breath, the moment before the vow.
6. **Reduce Until It Hurts** --- If an element can be removed without losing meaning, remove it. Restraint is the signal of confidence.
7. **Hand-Placed Feel** --- Subtle rotations, staggered reveals, asymmetric details. Nothing feels machine-generated. Everything feels considered.
8. **Motion as Breath** --- Animations are slow, deliberate, and tied to emotional pacing. 180ms for hover, 700ms for reveals, 25-35s for Ken Burns drifts. Nothing fast. Nothing flashy.
9. **Photography Over Illustration** --- All imagery is photographic: piano keys, candlelight, ceremony setups, intimate moments. Shallow depth of field. Warm desaturated tones. Never posed. Never stock.
10. **The Process is Visible** --- The collaborative journey (Listening, Crafting, Refining, Completing) must be architecturally present on the page, not hidden in a FAQ.

### Color Logic
- **Charcoal/Rich Black (88-92%):** The vigil. The foundation. The discipline of preparation.
- **Warm Cream/Taupe (surface sections):** The exhale. The celebration. Where content is primary.
- **Vow Yellow / hsl(45 90% 65%) (max 6%):** Sacred accents only. CTAs, underlines, the semicolon, golden threads. Overuse dilutes its power entirely.
- **Vine Green / hsl(100 65% 60%) (max 4%):** Success states, availability indicators only. Never decorative.

### Typography Direction
- **Display/Headings:** Cormorant Garamond --- high-contrast serif. Light weight (300-400). Used for headlines, sacred quotes, package names. It whispers authority.
- **Body/Interface:** Inter --- clean, modern sans-serif. Regular weight. Used for descriptions, features, navigation. It provides clarity without competing.
- **Scale:** 9 locked sizes. No exceptions. Hierarchy through weight and spacing, not size proliferation.

### Photography/Art Direction Rules
- Shallow depth of field, warm color grading (desaturated, slight sepia lift)
- Subjects: piano keys, candlelight, ceremony setups, hands on keys, empty venues before guests arrive
- Never: posed couple shots looking at camera, bright flash, generic wedding stock
- Opacity: Background images at 6-15% opacity, never competing with content
- Ken Burns drift on all background images (25-35s cycles)
- All images must feel like they were taken by someone who was already in the room --- not by someone who arrived to photograph

### Anti-Patterns (what makes the brand look generic)
- Bright, saturated hero images with smiling couples
- Stock photos of any kind
- Grid layouts with equal-sized cards and no visual hierarchy
- Blue or teal accent colors
- Sans-serif headings
- Rounded corners larger than 8px
- Drop shadows without atmospheric context
- Animated counters or scrolling number tickers
- "Before and after" sliders
- Hamburger menus on desktop
- Any element that draws attention to itself rather than serving the content

## 11. What Not to Do (Hard Guardrails)

1. **Never say "we."** This is a sole practitioner brand. "We" implies a company. "I" implies devotion.
2. **Never use urgency tactics.** No countdown timers, no "only X dates left" unless verifiably true for a stated season with real numbers.
3. **Never position as entertainment.** This is ceremony sound direction. The word "entertainment" does not appear.
4. **Never use stock photography.** All imagery is either real ceremony shots or AI-generated atmospheric imagery matching the brand's cinematic warmth.
5. **Never compare directly to competitors in copy.** The Transformation section mirrors fears and resolves them. It does not name DJs or other pianists.
6. **Never use the word "package."** Use "path" or "presence." Packages are commodities. Paths are choices.
7. **Never break the emotional arc.** The page flows: Mystery, Recognition, Understanding, Desire, Commitment. Inserting a feature dump or FAQ in the middle breaks the spell.
8. **Never sacrifice performance for visual effect.** All images lazy-loaded. All animations GPU-composited. No layout shifts.
9. **Never let the design feel effortful.** The visitor should feel held, not impressed. If they notice the design before they feel the emotion, it has failed.
10. **Never change the tagline.** "'Til Death; Unto Life" is a covenant. It does not get A/B tested.
11. **Never claim "500+ weddings."** The credential is "500+ events." Precision protects trust.
12. **Never reference Banff Mode, Banff venues, or Parks Canada.** This capability does not exist in the brand.
13. **Never use "performer," "entertainer," "vendor," or "service provider."** Parker is a ceremony pianist and sound director.
14. **Never let the process be invisible.** The months-long collaborative preparation is the core differentiator. It must be shown, not assumed.
15. **Never rush the website experience.** The visitor's journey mirrors the ceremony: vigil, then exhale, then understanding, then commitment. Skipping steps breaks trust.

## 12. Brand Archetype

### Primary: The Sage-Caregiver Hybrid

**Sage Attributes (knowledge, preparation, mastery):**
- 17 years of musical depth
- Full ceremony choreography --- every cue mapped
- Run-of-show documentation, SPL logging, triple redundancy
- "No assumptions. I ask."

**Caregiver Attributes (devotion, service, emotional presence):**
- Begins preparation the week of booking
- Stays until the room is empty
- Multiple refinement rounds guided by the couple's voice
- "To let my music sound like what your hearts feel like"

**Why This Hybrid:**
The Sage alone feels detached and clinical. The Caregiver alone feels soft and lacks authority. Together, they create the exact brand personality Parker embodies: someone who knows deeply and cares completely. The Sage provides the months of preparation; the Caregiver provides the emotional translation. The couple receives both mastery and devotion.

**Risk to Manage:**
The Sage can become preachy. The Caregiver can become self-sacrificing. The brand must remain quietly confident, never lecturing, never martyred. The voice is composed --- not teacherly. The devotion is professional --- not desperate.
