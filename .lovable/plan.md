

# Plan: Teaching Vertical — Brand Identity Prompt Engineering

This is not a code change — it is the creation of a world-class, Fantasy.co-grade brand identity prompt specifically engineered for the **Teaching** vertical of Parker Gawryletz's piano business. This prompt will serve as the North Star document for building the teaching website, just as `.lovable/wedding-brand-identity.md` governs the weddings vertical.

The deliverable is a new file: `.lovable/teaching-brand-identity.md`

---

## What This Document Must Accomplish

The weddings brand identity is 617 lines of meticulously crafted governance. The teaching prompt must match that depth and rigor, but with a fundamentally different emotional architecture. Weddings are about **witnessing sacred moments**. Teaching is about **unlocking someone's relationship with the instrument**. The ceremony pianist carries vows. The teacher carries potential.

---

## Key Brand Decisions Pre-Loaded Into the Prompt

### The Emotional Architecture Shift

**Weddings = Death → Life (vigil → covenant)**
The emotional arc is gravity → release. The visitor walks through darkness into light. The held breath becomes the exhale.

**Teaching = Silence → Sound (potential → expression)**
The emotional arc is emptiness → fullness. The student arrives with unexpressed music inside them. Parker draws it out. The silence before the first note becomes the music that follows. The teaching site breathes between **stillness** (the unplayed piano, the empty bench, the waiting keys) and **resonance** (the moment the student's hands find the right chord, the first time a piece comes alive).

### Category Ownership

Not "piano lessons." Not "piano teacher." **Piano Mentorship** — a long-term relationship where Parker doesn't teach technique in isolation; he teaches the student to speak through the instrument. The category name must feel as elevated as "Ceremony Sound Direction."

### The Enemy

The 30-minute lesson factory. YouTube tutorials that teach finger positions but not feeling. The teacher who assigns grades and exercises but never asks "what do you want to say with this instrument?" The entire system that treats piano as a skill to acquire rather than a voice to discover.

### The Promise

"I will teach you to play the piano the way you hear it inside your head — with patience, with intention, and with the understanding that the instrument is not something to conquer but something to converse with."

### The Audience

- Adults who always wanted to learn but were told it was "too late"
- Children whose parents want them to love music, not just pass exams
- Intermediate players who can read notes but can't yet speak through the keys
- People who want to play for their wedding, their partner, their own healing
- Anyone who believes music is a language worth learning to speak

### Voice Adaptation

The teaching voice is **warmer** than the wedding voice. Still composed. Still first-person. Still no exclamation marks. But the reverence shifts from "the weight of a sacred vow" to "the patience of becoming." The teacher's voice is unhurried, encouraging without being saccharine, and honest about the difficulty of the journey.

**Wedding voice:** "I carry every vow so it lands where it belongs."
**Teaching voice:** "I sit beside you until the music sounds the way you hear it inside."

### Visual Adaptation

The teaching vertical shares the same design system (Fitzgerald spacing, Cormorant/Inter, sacred objects, atmospheric depth) but with a warmer baseline temperature:

- **Less charcoal, more warm cream** — the vigil is lighter because teaching is not about death/life; it's about patience/discovery
- **Vow-yellow remains** but represents the moment of breakthrough, not the threshold of commitment
- **Photography shifts:** piano keys with hands (student hands, not performer hands), natural light practice rooms, close-ups of sheet music with pencil annotations, the empty piano bench waiting
- **The semicolon still appears** but its meaning shifts: the pause between not-knowing and knowing, between silence and sound

## Content of the Prompt Document

The document will contain approximately 600-700 lines covering:

### Section 1: Brand Truth Table
Facts about Parker's teaching practice — years of experience, teaching philosophy, student demographics, lesson format, geographic availability (in-person Calgary + virtual), pricing model, scheduling approach.

### Section 2: Non-Negotiables
- First-person voice ("I teach," never "we offer lessons")
- No grade-based or exam-focused language
- No "learn piano fast" or speed promises
- The relationship is mentorship, not instruction
- Patience is positioned as methodology, not marketing

### Section 3: Brand Spine
- **Category:** Piano Mentorship
- **Enemy:** The lesson factory. The YouTube shortcut. The teacher who assigns exercises without asking what the student wants to say.
- **Audience:** Romantics of sound — adults who always wanted to play, parents who want their children to love music, intermediate players seeking depth
- **Promise:** "I will sit beside you until the music sounds the way you hear it inside."
- **Proof:** 17 years playing, 500+ events, teaching experience, patience-first methodology
- **Personality:** Patient. Warm. Quietly demanding. Present. Encouraging without flattery.

### Section 4: Positioning
Parker is not competing with music schools or app-based learning. He occupies the space where personal mentorship, emotional connection to the instrument, and long-term growth converge.

### Section 5: Customer Identity Mirror
"You are my student if you believe the piano has something to say that only your hands can say."
"Not for you if you want to learn 'Happy Birthday' in three lessons and move on."

### Section 6: Brand Story System
**Narrative spine:** The student arrives with a song inside them they cannot yet play. Through months of patient work, that song emerges — not as a technical achievement, but as an act of self-expression. The transformation is from silence to sound, from listening to speaking, from hearing music to making it.

### Section 7: Messaging Pillars
1. **Patient Mentorship** — "I don't teach speed. I teach depth."
2. **Emotional Fluency** — "The piano is not a skill. It is a voice."
3. **Lifelong Relationship** — "There is no graduation. Only growing."

### Section 8: Verbal Identity
Voice traits table, lexicon (words owned: mentorship, patience, voice, fluency, resonance; words avoided: lessons, class, student body, beginner/advanced labels as judgments), writing standards, do/don't examples.

### Section 9: Visual Identity Direction
Warmer palette baseline, photography direction (practice rooms, hands on keys, pencil-annotated sheet music, natural light), motion philosophy adapted for teaching (slower, more contemplative).

### Section 10: Brand Governance
Decision filter adapted: "Does this honor the student's pace?" / "Does this feel like mentorship, not instruction?" / "Does this tell Parker's teaching story?"

### Section 11: What Not to Do
Never promise speed. Never use grade language. Never show exam certificates. Never position as "affordable lessons." Never use "beginner" as a label. Never compare to apps or YouTube.

### Section 12: Brand Archetype
**The Sage-Guide Hybrid** — The Sage provides depth of knowledge. The Guide walks beside the student rather than lecturing from above. Together: mastery + companionship.
