

# Homepage Copy Elevation -- Section-by-Section

## Philosophy

The homepage already has several lines that are genuinely world-class -- "You're about to make a promise that will echo beyond your lifetime," "To let my music sound like what your hearts feel like," and "Because there are no second chances for First Moments." These are sacred text. They stay.

The goal is to elevate the remaining copy to match that standard -- replacing generic service language with first-person, emotionally specific, covenant-level prose. Every line must pass the North Star test: "Could any vendor say this, or is it uniquely mine?"

No design changes. No layout changes. Only words.

## Section 3: The Process (ProcessSection.tsx)

### Intro Block

**Current:**
```
Label: "The Process"
Headline: "Excellence on the big day doesn't happen on the big day."
Highlight: "It happens now."
Bridge: "This is my process for ensuring it happens every time."
Statement: "Because there are no second chances for First Moments"
```

**Verdict:** The headline and statement are sacred. The bridge line is functional but slightly generic -- "ensuring it happens every time" reads like a warranty, not a covenant.

**Proposed:**
- Bridge: "This is how I prepare for the moment that matters most." (Shifts from mechanical guarantee to emotional preparation; keeps first-person voice)

### Movement I -- THE LISTENING

**Current:**
```
Action: "I ask"
Quote: "Before I play a single note, I learn your story."
Details: "What song was playing when you knew? What tempo matches the way your heart beats when you think about walking toward them?"
Assumption: "I don't assume I know. I ask."
Outcome: "We begin with a conversation."
```

**Verdict:** Strong. The "What song was playing when you knew?" question is emotionally precise. Minor polish only.

**Proposed changes:**
- Details: "What song was playing when you knew? What does your heart sound like when you think about walking toward them?" (Removes "tempo matches" -- too technical for this emotional moment; connects directly to the brand mission of translating what hearts feel like)
- Outcome: "It begins with a conversation." (Removes "We" -- the couple initiates; "It" honors the process as something larger than either party)

### Movement II -- THE CRAFTING

**Current:**
```
Action: "I create"
Quote: "Then I disappear into your vision."
Details: "Note by note. Measure by measure. Your walk-down song--not selected from a list, but composed from our conversation."
Assumption: "I don't assume a cover will capture it. I create."
Outcome: "Custom arrangement. Your love story, translated."
```

**Verdict:** "Then I disappear into your vision" is excellent. "Custom arrangement. Your love story, translated." is strong but reads slightly like a tagline. Polish the outcome.

**Proposed changes:**
- Outcome: "Your love story, translated into sound." (Adds "into sound" -- connects back to the core mission and completes the metaphor)

### Movement III -- THE REFINING

**Current:**
```
Action: "I refine"
Quote: "I send you a first draft--raw, unpolished, honest."
Details: "Not to impress you. To ask you: 'Am I heading the right direction?' If something feels off, we course-correct. Your feedback isn't inconvenient. It's essential."
Assumption: "I don't assume I got it right. I check."
Outcome: "We iterate until it sounds exactly like you imagined."
```

**Verdict:** Nearly perfect. "Your feedback isn't inconvenient. It's essential." is excellent tension-resolution. One minor polish.

**Proposed changes:**
- Details: "Not to impress you. To ask: 'Does this sound like you?' If something feels off, we course-correct. Your feedback is not inconvenient--it is essential." (Replaces "Am I heading the right direction?" with "Does this sound like you?" -- more emotionally direct, ties back to the mission of translating feelings into sound; en-dash formatting per brand voice rules)

### Movement IV -- THE COMPLETING

**Current:**
```
Action: "We complete"
Quote: "Now we fill the rest of the air together."
Details: "Prelude. Procession. Cocktails. Dinner. You brainstorm, or I suggest--either way, we decide together. Communication all the way through. No one left wondering. No silence where there should be sound."
Assumption: "I don't assume you know what you want. I guide and ask."
Outcome: "Every note intentional. Every decision yours."
```

**Verdict:** "No silence where there should be sound" is a standout line. The rest is solid. Minor refinement to the assumption line.

**Proposed changes:**
- Assumption: "I don't assume you know what you need. I guide, and I ask." (Changes "want" to "need" -- more respectful; adds comma before "and I ask" for rhythm per Oxford comma rule)

### Closing Block

**Current:**
```
Promise: "Because there's one chance to get this right."
Assurance: "And it will be right."
CTA: "Begin the conversation"
```

**Verdict:** Strong. No changes needed.

## Section 5: The Invitation (TheInvitation.tsx)

**Current:**
```
Label: "The Invitation"
Headline: "Before I play a single note, I listen."
Body 1: "I've been at 200+ ceremonies. I've seen what happens when the wind takes the vows. When the generator hums through the 'I do.' When the back row strains to hear."
Body 2: "I built a system so that never happens to you."
Caption: "60 seconds on what I do--and why it matters."
Link: "Read my full story"
```

**Verdict:** The headline duplicates the Listening movement's quote ("Before I play a single note, I learn your story"). This creates narrative redundancy. The body copy is effective but "I built a system" reads like engineering, not witnessing.

**Proposed changes:**
- Headline: "I have been at 200 ceremonies. I know what can go wrong." (Moves the credential into the headline where it creates immediate authority; uses the brand's composed, declarative voice; removes the "listen" echo)
- Body 1: "The wind that carries vows away. The generator that hums through the 'I do.' The back row that strains and gives up." (Transforms from telling to showing -- each line is a vivid scene, not a reported event; creates emotional tension through specificity)
- Body 2: "I designed every part of my process so that never happens to you." (Replaces "built a system" with "designed every part of my process" -- more personal, connects back to the Process section above)
- Caption: "60 seconds on what I do--and why." (Trims "it matters" -- confidence doesn't explain itself)

## Section 6: The Sound (TheSound.tsx)

**Current:**
```
Label: "The Sound"
Headline: "Music that holds the room still."
Subhead: "These are real ceremony moments--prelude, processional, vows, recession."
Coming soon: "Samples arriving soon"
Closing: "Every arrangement crafted specifically for the couple. No two ceremonies sound alike."
```

**Verdict:** "Music that holds the room still" is strong. The subhead is explanatory and breaks the spell. The closing is generic.

**Proposed changes:**
- Subhead: "The prelude. The procession. The vows. The walk into forever." (Transforms a list into a narrative sequence; "walk into forever" replaces "recession" which is industry jargon)
- Closing: "Every arrangement begins with a conversation--and ends with a sound that belongs only to you." (Connects back to the Listening movement and the core mission; "belongs only to you" reinforces exclusivity)

## Section 7: The Transformation (TheTransformation.tsx)

### Left Panel (Fears)

**Current:**
```
"Guests in the back can't hear our vows"
"Generators hum through the ceremony"
"No SPL proof for permit compliance"
"DJ arrives with no ceremony mic plan"
```

**Verdict:** These are specific and effective fears. "No SPL proof for permit compliance" is too technical for the emotional context.

**Proposed changes:**
- Fear 3: "No proof the sound reached every seat" (Translates the technical SPL concept into an emotional fear -- the couple worries about guests hearing, not about compliance paperwork)
- Fear 4: "The musician arrives with no plan for your ceremony" (Changes "DJ" to "musician" -- broader; adds "your" to make it personal)

### Right Panel (Resolutions)

**Current:**
```
"Every guest hears--mic coverage documented"
"Silent battery power; zero ambient noise"
"3 SPL readings logged with timestamps"
"Ceremony audio plan delivered in 24 hours"
```

**Verdict:** These read like spec sheets. They need to answer the fears emotionally while keeping the proof points.

**Proposed changes:**
- Resolution 1: "Every guest hears every word--documented" (Simplifies; "every word" connects to the vow theme)
- Resolution 2: "Silent power. No generators. No hum." (Shorter, more decisive; mirrors the rhythmic cadence of the brand)
- Resolution 3: "Three sound readings logged and time-stamped" (More readable than "3 SPL readings"; removes jargon)
- Resolution 4: "Your ceremony audio plan--delivered within 24 hours" (Adds "Your" for ownership; en-dash per brand voice)

## Section 8: The Witness (TheWitness.tsx)

**Current:**
```
Label: "THE WITNESS"
Headline: "Not a musician--your ceremony witness."
Declarations: "I arrive an hour early." / "I test your officiant's mic." / "I co-sign your cue sheet."
Kit label: "Standard Kit"
```

**Verdict:** "Not a musician--your ceremony witness" is strong brand positioning. The declarations are good but could be more emotionally resonant. "Standard Kit" is generic.

**Proposed changes:**
- Declaration 1: "I arrive before anyone else." (More atmospheric than a specific time; creates the image of solitary preparation -- the vigil)
- Declaration 2: "I test every microphone myself." (Removes "officiant's" -- broader; "myself" reinforces personal accountability)
- Declaration 3: "I walk your cue sheet until it is second nature." (Transforms from "co-sign" (transactional) to "walk... until second nature" (devotional))
- Kit label: "What I bring" (First-person, warmer, matches brand voice)

## Section 9: Three Paths / Your Presence (ThreePaths.tsx)

**Current:**
```
Label: "YOUR PRESENCE"
Headline: "How deeply do you want me there?"
Subhead: "Choose the level of ceremony audio coverage that matches your day."
Reassurance: "Upgrade anytime. No penalty until 2 weeks prior."
CTA (all cards): "Choose this presence"
```

**Verdict:** "How deeply do you want me there?" is excellent -- it frames packages as depth of relationship, not tiers of service. The subhead undermines it by reverting to "ceremony audio coverage."

**Proposed changes:**
- Subhead: "Three ways I can be present on your day." (Maintains the "presence" framing; avoids "coverage" which sounds like insurance)
- Reassurance: "You can move between these at any time--no penalty until two weeks before your day." (More conversational; spells out "two weeks" per specificity rule; "your day" instead of "prior")

Note: All pricing details, package names, descriptions, and feature lists remain exactly as they are per user request.
