

# Phase 5: Teaching Page Copy Overhaul — Grounded & Direct

## Problem

The current teaching page copy is deeply philosophical and poetic — "sacred," "vigil," "bench remembers everyone," "the silence holds before you give it shape." While beautiful, it reads more like spiritual literature than a piano teacher's website. A prospective student (likely an adult, possibly nervous) needs to quickly understand: what Parker offers, how it works, what it costs, and why it feels different. The copy needs to stay warm and personal but become **concrete, approachable, and human-scaled**.

## Guiding Principles for the Rewrite

- **First person, conversational** — Parker talking directly to you, not reciting poetry
- **Specific over abstract** — "60-minute sessions" not "the bench awaits"
- **Acknowledge real feelings simply** — "It is normal to feel nervous" not "what the silence holds before you give it shape"
- **Keep the brand warmth** — still patient, still personal, just not mystical
- **Preserve the visual/animation system** — only changing text content, not scroll reveals or layout

## Section-by-Section Copy Changes

### 1. TeachingHero.tsx
**Current:** "From Silence; Unto Sound." / "I sit beside you until what you hear inside finds its way through your hands."
**Proposed:** "Piano Mentorship" (label stays) / Main tagline: "Learn piano at your own pace." / Subtitle: "Patient, one-on-one lessons for adults and beginners. No grades. No pressure. Just music."
- Remove "Scroll to sit down" → "Scroll to learn more"

### 2. TeachingExhale.tsx
**Current:** "There is a sound within you that your hands have not yet reached..." / "The bench is waiting."
**Proposed lines:**
- "Maybe you played as a child and stopped. Maybe you have never touched a key."
- "Either way, something brought you here — and that is enough to begin."
- "I will meet you where you are."
- "Your first lesson starts with a conversation."
- Section label: "My philosophy" (stays) → or change to "Where we start"
- Closing annotation: "— you have heard it all along" → "— no experience required"

### 3. TeachingPillars.tsx
**Current pillars with philosophical language:**
- "Patience as Practice" / "Your pace is the curriculum..."
- "Voice Before Technique" / "The piano is not a skill to acquire. It is a voice waiting to be uncovered..."
- "Freedom Through Fluency" / "When your hands know the instrument deeply enough, they stop translating..."
- "No Graduation" / "There is no final lesson. Only deepening..."

**Proposed pillars (same structure, grounded language):**

| Pillar | Title | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| I | Your Pace, Always | There are no deadlines, no exams, no fixed curriculum. We move at whatever speed feels right for you — whether that is one new piece a month or spending three sessions on a single passage. |
| II | Expression Over Perfection | I care more about how the music makes you feel than whether every note is correct. Technique matters, but it serves the music — not the other way around. |
| III | Real Songs, Not Just Exercises | You will play music you actually want to play. We work on pieces you choose — hymns, film scores, pop songs, classical — alongside the fundamentals that make them possible. |
| IV | No End Date | This is not a 12-week course. Some students have been with me for years. Others come for a season. The mentorship lasts as long as it is useful to you. |

- Section label: "What I hold to be true" → "How I teach"
- Closing annotation: "— four convictions, one bench" → "— these guide every lesson"

### 4. TeachingMethodology.tsx (stays dark — "The First Conversation")
**Current:** '"What do you want to say through this instrument?"' / "The first question I ask is never about music. It is about you. What brought you to the bench. What you hear when no one is listening..."
**Proposed:** '"What kind of music do you want to play?"' / "The first lesson is really a conversation. I want to know what brought you to the piano — what songs you love, what you hope to be able to do, and what has held you back. We start there, not with scales."
- Seed metaphor: "Every voice arrives as a seed..." → "From that conversation, we build a plan that is yours — not a syllabus I hand to everyone."
- Pencil annotation: "— listen before you play" → "— we start by talking"

### 5. TeachingThreshold.tsx (stays dark — fear/resolution pairs)
**Current fears are good but resolutions are overwrought.** Simplify:

| Fear | Current Resolution (truncated) | Proposed Resolution |
|------|------|------|
| "What if I start and quit again?" | "You did not quit. You were taught by someone who measured your progress by their timeline..." | "Most of my students tried lessons before and stopped. That is not failure — it usually means the approach was wrong. Here, there is no timeline and no pressure. We go at your pace." |
| "What if I never play anything real?" | "The first real thing you play will not be a concerto..." | "You will play real music from the start — not just exercises. It might be simple at first, but it will be something you chose and something you enjoy." |
| "What if it feels like lessons?" | "There is no curriculum. No homework. No grades..." | "There is no homework, no grading, no recitals unless you want them. It feels more like a weekly conversation that happens to involve a piano." |
| "What if I'm too old to start?" | "There is no age at which the piano stops being willing to listen..." | "Most of my students are adults. I have taught people who started at 30, 50, and 70. The piano does not care when you begin — and neither do I." |

- Section label: "What you carry to the bench" → "Common concerns"
- Closing annotation: "— you arrived exactly now" → "— these are normal to feel"

| Margaret | "Margaret started at 52. She had never played before and apologised constantly in the first few weeks. Six months in, she played a piece for her husband on their anniversary. It was not perfect — but it was hers." | "The first gift I ever made with my own hands." |

- Closing annotation: "— every voice began at the bench" → "— real students, real moments"
