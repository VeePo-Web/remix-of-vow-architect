## 2. Non-Negotiables / Flex / Dealbreakers

### Non-Negotiables
- First-person voice ("I" — never "we" or "Parker Allard provides")
- Professional and polished tone — confident without being stiff
- No exclamation marks — ever
- Frame live music as atmosphere and presence, not entertainment or performance
- Verb-forward CTAs ("Discuss your event," "Explore my repertoire" — never "Submit" or "Book now!")
- The semicolon as brand mark carries across all verticals
- Oxford comma — always

### Flex (Adapt Per Context)
- Warmth level can increase for church services and celebrations vs. corporate receptions
- Repertoire emphasis shifts by occasion (hymns for church, pop/film for dinners, classical for receptions)
- Photography style can lean warmer for intimate events, cooler for formal ones
- Price framing can reference "investment" or simply state figures — no discounting language

### Dealbreakers — Never Do This
- Never use sacred/vigil/covenant language from the weddings vertical
- Never use mentorship/growth language from the teaching vertical
- Never frame music as "background" or "filler"
- Never use "DJ," "performer," "entertainer," or "act" to describe myself
- Never use urgency tactics ("Limited spots!", "Book before it's too late!")
- Never use bright adjectives ("amazing," "incredible," "stunning")
- Never say "500+ weddings" — the correct claim is "500+ events"

## 5. Customer Identity Mirror

### You are my people if...
- You've hosted an event where the playlist felt hollow
- You want music that elevates conversation, not competes with it
- You care about the feeling of a room as much as the logistics
- You understand the difference between a musician who performs and one who is present

### Not for you if...

- You need a full band setup with amplification
- You're looking for the cheapest option
- You want someone to MC, announce, or be the center of attention
- You need music genres outside piano repertoire (electronic, heavy rock, etc.)

## 6. Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Atmosphere
> "I understand what live music does to a room."

Live piano creates something a playlist cannot — presence. Not sound filling space, but music that shapes the feeling of being together. I read the room's energy and respond in real time.

**Proof points:** 500+ events, real-time repertoire adaptation, volume that supports conversation.

### Pillar 2: Adaptability
> "Your event. Your sound."

Every gathering is different. A candlelit dinner needs something different from a church service, which needs something different from a cocktail reception. I carry 500+ songs and curate the set to your occasion, your guests, your atmosphere.

**Proof points:** 500+ song repertoire, 4 occasion types, pre-event consultation to align on tone.

### Pillar 3: Professionalism
> "I arrive early. I set up quietly. I leave only the memory."

No drama. No diva. Insured, punctual, dress-code adaptable, self-sufficient. I handle my own setup and teardown. You focus on your guests — I focus on the sound.

**Proof points:** Insurance documentation, early arrival protocol, zero-footprint setup.

## 8. Visual Direction

### How Events Differs from Other Verticals

| Aspect | Weddings | Teaching | Events |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dominant mood | Sacred, vigil, held breath | Warm, mentorship, growth | Professional, atmospheric, polished |
| Color temperature | Cold → warm transition | Warm throughout | Neutral-warm, amber accents |
| Hero treatment | 8s vigil sequence, flame | Empty bench, silence | Ken Burns on venue/piano, immediate reveal |
| Typography weight | Ultra-light, reverent | Warm serif, approachable | Clean, confident, moderate weight |
| Section rhythm | Inhale/exhale (dark/light) | Silence → sound spectrum | Steady rhythm, subtle alternation |
| Sacred objects | Semicolon, flame, golden thread | Semicolon, bench, pencil | Semicolon, golden dot (breathing) |
| Photography | Ceremony, candlelight, altar | Studio, bench, hands | Venue interiors, piano in room, ambient light |

### Events-Specific Visual Rules
- Hero reveals immediately (400ms) — no vigil delay
- Background image opacity kept low (0.14) for sophistication
- Grain overlay at 8% — present but not dominant
- Vignette gradient centers attention without drama
- Vow-yellow used sparingly — golden dot, period accent, CTA hover
- Section backgrounds alternate between rich-black and warm charcoal
- Cards use subtle border treatments, not heavy shadows
- Motion is smooth and professional — no dramatic reveals

## 9. Cross-Vertical Coherence

### Shared Elements Across All Verticals
- First-person voice
- Semicolon as brand mark

- Vine-green for success/availability states (≤4%)
- Cormorant display typeface

- Verb-forward CTAs
- Oxford comma
- Footer and navigation structure

### How Events Connects to the Broader Brand
The Sacred Sound brand operates on the principle that music carries weight — in weddings, that weight is sacred; in teaching, it is transformative; in events, it is atmospheric. The through-line is **intentionality**: every note serves a purpose.

Events is the most accessible vertical — the gateway for many clients who later book weddings or teaching. The tone is warmer and more immediately inviting than weddings, more professional than teaching, but shares the same underlying conviction: **live music, played with intention, changes the feeling of a room.**

### Vertical Navigation Language
- Weddings → "The Sacred Sound" (ceremony sound direction)
- Events → "The Sound" (private event pianist)
- Teaching → "The Silence" (piano mentorship)
