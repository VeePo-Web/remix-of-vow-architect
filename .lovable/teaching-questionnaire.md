## Section 1: The Core Vision

### 1.1 What is your core vision for piano mentorship?

I am wanting to build people. Above anything I am wanting to build people. My ambition is deep, not wide. So I may only take on 10 students at a time. But I want to go deep with them and not wide. I see the piano as a tool. Like a hammer. But instead of building a house, you are architecting what the soul looks like with, but with sound. This is my philosophy. So I want to train people and give them the skill to do this. I want to help them with their goals. When someone wants to learn an instrument there is always a deep meaning behind it deep down because deep down, music and instruments are inherently spiritual.

### 1.2 What is the website ultimately meant to do in the life of your practice?

Attract the right people. Express a philosophy. This is about the art. I provide the service but this is about capturing the art behind it. And the depth. Music and piano is sacred to me, and I want to be able to give that gift to other people of learning to play the piano.

### 1.3 What is the clearest articulation of the vision behind Piano Mentorship as you see it today?

I see piano mentorship as I am giving someone a tool for them to be able to express what is in their soul. It is like an extension of themselves. I see the piano as a body in the sense that the body is here for a big reason to express what is in our soul. Our soul is happy, we smile, our soul is sad, we cry. The piano is the same. But it's not through a smile, it is through an entirely different system of expression. Like a major chord or something. Music can reach the extremes of every single emotion and human expression and it transcends words.

### 1.4 What are you trying to protect this practice from becoming?

I don't want it to be a transactional service. I don't want it to be a shallow money-making business. I don't want it to be scalable in the sense of going wide. Maybe in going deep. I want to protect the sacredness of music and passing that on. I want to work wholeheartedly with each student I have to help them reach their goals.

### 1.5 What is the category you believe you are creating, claiming, or redefining through this work?

I am creating a category of its own. I have not been taught how to teach piano. So I am pioneering a new way. That isn't about scaling or the business. I don't need to teach to survive, so I am free to do it out of depth and not desperation. I am not forced to do it, I do it from the fuel of passion.

### 1.6 What would it mean for the website to express that category successfully?

For the website to express that successfully would mean that all visuals, all copy, all pictures, all sounds, all animations express that. Each little detail needs to reflect that. The website itself needs to be alive with this personality. The deep and not wide. The not desperate. But just putting your truth out there and polarizing the ones who come with you into the vision. Who are captivated by the vision.

### 1.7 What truths about your work feel difficult to communicate, but essential to communicate well?

Some truths that feel difficult are the truths that piano has a very steep learning curve. And being honest there will be times it won't feel fun to practice. But we work on specifically discerning for you what makes it worth it. I think also the truth that piano is more than music.

### 1.8 When someone finishes reading the website, what do you want them to understand that they did not understand before?

I want them to understand my specific philosophy on teaching and on music and on instruments and the piano. I want them to know my exclusivity, and my prices, and my style of teaching, and my process.

## Section 2: Meaning, Philosophy, and Interior Logic

### 2.1 What is the philosophical foundation beneath this practice?

That every single person has the potential to become great. It doesn't matter who you are, every single person has the potential to become great. The ones who don't stick with it don't have a strong "WHY." So I spend a lot of time not just on the skills of the piano but on the deeper side with each individual. And each person is unique and I want to hear their stories of why they want to learn piano. What thought or vision brings them life when they think of themselves playing piano. Who do they imagine themselves playing in front of? In what context?

### 2.2 What do you believe most people misunderstand about learning piano?

I think people will think it will be easy and linear. It is not easy. I think people think that learning the piano will be super fun. A lot of it is not fun. A lot of it is very robotic and repetitive. But it is all learning how to, which is good.

### 2.3 What do you believe most teachers, schools, or platforms get wrong about the relationship between a student and the instrument?

I believe they don't focus on the deeper reasons. I think they don't focus on the individual. They focus on reading music, and reading sheet music. But that is the expression of what is in someone else's heart. Not their own. It can feel relatable. But even when they are playing someone else's sheet music it needs to be authentic to their own identity and to architect what is in their own soul.

### 2.4 Why is the word "mentorship" non-negotiable in this practice?

It is because it is relational and it is organic and I am with them in a deeper way than just on their piano lesson. I cater the lessons specifically for them and create questions to help them specifically flesh out their vision and their why. And to give them their own unique voice through the piano.

### 2.5 Why is this not "teaching" in the conventional sense?

Because it is not just information transfer. But it is shaping the person who is learning as well.

### 2.6 What is the role of patience in your work beyond temperament or personality?

We understand that failure is inevitable and sometimes the only way up is down. Like a hike, the path up is not linear. And the formation is in the frustration but the perseverance of that frustration. It is not in the destination. We stare at the destination and we persevere until we get there.

### 2.7 What is the role of expression in your methodology?

My role of expression is that the piano is a tool for expression. And each person playing the piano will be unique in crafting "their sound" because the person is unique. I see it as language and words. In the sense Jesus said that out of the heart the mouth speaks. So a word is only a reflection of the heart. I see that the same. Ideally, out of the heart, we play the piano and what we play is simply a reflection of what is in our heart.

### 2.8 What is the role of conversation before music?

To understand and grip their vision. This is their north star and it needs to happen first because it will be what anchors them. It is their light at the end of the tunnel.

### 2.9 What does "I sit beside you until the music sounds the way you hear it inside" mean operationally, emotionally, and symbolically?

It means that we understand that there will be friction. But we also understand that with practice, and even biologically how the brain works, it will come a point where it feels frictionless. And second nature where you just play and you don't need to think twice about it, but you just play.

### 2.10 What part of your vision feels the most sacred, and therefore the easiest to dilute if the website is handled superficially?

The philosophy of music. That it is so intricate because it reflects the human soul. And the human soul is so intricate. But it is so beautiful because in all of its intricacies, somehow it is still relatable.

## Section 3: Identity, Positioning, and Distinction

### 3.1 What makes this practice fundamentally different from a music school?

We go deep. It is mentorship. It is not shallow. It helps the individual. They aren't put into a system. They are put with a teacher who is organic and relational. It is discovery, not mission. And it is relational, not a system.

### 3.2 What makes it fundamentally different from a typical private piano teacher?

That I put in a lot of work outside of the allotted lesson time. To make it custom and bespoke to each student. And that I don't teach the traditional route of just following the Royal Conservatory book. I make my own pathway unique for each student. And if that means the Royal Conservatory then that is great. But it is about adapting to the student's learning style.

### 3.3 What makes it fundamentally different from apps, YouTube instruction, or self-guided learning?

It is relational and gives direct specific feedback, not from a system or a program but from a person with experience. They are walking through a maze in first person. But I have walked through that maze and can offer guidance from a bird's eye view. Guiding them.

### 3.4 What are the distinctions the website must draw with absolute clarity?

- This is not a music school
- This is not exam preparation
- This is not curriculum-driven instruction
- This is not group teaching
- This is not a transactional lesson model
- This is not a scaled education business

### 3.5 What do you want the right person to feel when they realize this is for them?

I want them to feel like this is specifically for them. I want them to feel safe. A lot of people feel anxious when first starting because they don't believe in themselves. I want to extinguish this. I want them to feel hope that they can truly achieve their goals and I will help them and not give up on them.

### 3.6 What do you want the wrong-fit person to understand quickly and clearly?

I want the wrong person to understand that it's not for them if they just want to be shallow. Or learn to read someone else's sheet music per se only.

### 3.7 What kind of person is most likely to resonate with this practice at a soul level?

Maybe people who want to write music. Or in worship to God. Or people who want to express themselves and learn. Or people who are deep and philosophical maybe. Or a parent who really cares for their kid. A kid who gets the deeper things in life.

### 3.8 What kind of person may appear interested on paper, but is not actually aligned with the work?

Someone who is not interested in putting in the work.

### 3.9 What tension or contradiction sits at the center of the brand?

It is gentle but rigorous. It is deep but fun. It is intense but unto reaching the goal. It is creative yet structured.

## Section 4: The Personal Brand Dimension

### 4.1 Why does this website need to be unmistakably personal to you?

Because my brand is unique and my teaching style and playing style is ultimately super unique.

### 4.2 What can only be communicated if the site is clearly centered on "I" rather than an abstract business voice?

They aren't plugging into a corporate system. It is not a daycare. But it is for the depth of the art of piano and learning it.

### 4.3 What role does your own life with the piano play in the authority of this brand?

When you own the land, you can build whatever you want on it. This meaning that when you own all the technique and master it in piano. All the theory and scales, you can accurately express what you want to. Even intricate stuff like the sound of childhood for example. For me music means so much. It is a way to tell my own story through writing songs, to help people. And helping other people scribe their songs and capture the depth of their songs, through producing for them.

### 4.4 Which parts of your personal story should shape the site most strongly?

- Your relationship to sacredness and sound
- Your own philosophy of patience and listening
- Your experience accompanying people through emotionally significant moments, and intricate moments, like weddings. Or for example, when you are playing piano at church, and you see someone's life being radically transformed. They are crying uncontrollably. How can you hold the sacredness of this? It is all about feel, and then the technical skill as well to be able to respond to what you feel in real time.

### 4.5 What should visitors understand about you within the first few moments on the site?

That piano is much deeper than people think it is. That it is more of a life philosophy than anything. In the same way that chess players look at it as a metaphor for life. Piano is the same.

### 4.6 What kind of trust should the site build around you specifically?

- Trust in your depth
- Trust in your clarity
- Especially trust in the structure of the lessons. A lot of the talk is abstract, so they need to trust my process and the structure. Depth without structure is not sustainable. Beauty without an iron base in foundation will not last.

### 4.7 What should never be lost, flattened, or generalized when translating your voice into web copy?

The structure of the piano lessons. And that primarily the piano lessons are about THEM, the student. Helping them reach their goals, whatever it is. I am only here to help build the bridge for them to reach their goals. And then they must walk on it.
